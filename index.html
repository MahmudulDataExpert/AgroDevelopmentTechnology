About Agro Dev

It goes without saying that the economic development of Bangladesh based on agriculture is particularly dependent on crop production. The food security of the country's 160 million people, the country's economic existence and the fate of the entire nation depend on the production of sufficient rice, jute, wheat, pulses, oilseeds, fruits and vegetables. Although the soil of this country is favorable for production, the yield of various crops is low. The role of disease is one of the various reasons for this low productivity. In case of disease, the health of the crop is damaged, the crop dies, thereby disrupting the production. Crop diseases are usually caused by microscopic fungi, bacteria, viruses, nematodes or worms. Those who cannot be seen with the naked eye without a microscope.
Almost every crop in Bangladesh is affected by various diseases. Every year a significant amount of crops are lost due to disease alone. To be specific, every year 10 to 15 percent of crops are lost due to diseases. Day by day the disease attack in our crops is increasing. The recent outbreak of wheat blast disease in the country as well as in Asia has surprised and alarmed us. That is why we need to give more importance and modernize the research and technology expansion related to crop diseases and its suppression management. Pesticides are not controlled due to not using the right pesticide, right time and right dose. Rather, it pollutes the environment, spoils soil health and pollutes reservoir water. Moreover, using excessive amounts of pesticides increases the resistance of the pest, so that pesticide no longer works. Then relatively more toxic and higher doses of pesticides have to be applied. As a result, on the one hand, the cost of production of crops increases, on the other hand, the risk to the environment and our health increases.
So we must first consider environmentally friendly ways to control crop diseases. Research on integrated pest management for disease control should be increased and such updated technologies should be made available to farmers. We hope that this website developed with this objective in mind will be useful for farmers, agriculturists and all those involved in agriculture. A big thank you to everyone involved in making it.
